List Types

There are two types of lists in html. The ordered list, and the unordered list

Unordered List

the < ul > tag is used for an unordered lists

Ordered List

the < ol > tag is used for an ordered lists

The Unordered List

the purpose, is to have a list of items of equal importance.

for example, a list of grocery items

  • eggs
  • milk
  • butter
  • bread
  • flour
  • fruit
  • vegtables

The Ordered List

the purpose, is to have a list of items of unequal importance. Normally highest importance first, least important last on the list

for example, the instruction/method of baking a cake

  1. Preheat oven to 350°.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk: flour, sugar, baking cocoa, baking powder, salt
  3. stir in eggs, oil, butter
  4. add water, stir until mixture is like a dough
  5. transfer mixture to a 2" pan
  6. bake in oven for 35-45minutes

The Nested List

there are times when a list-within-a-list is required. This is called a nested list.

take the two examples above

in the grocery list, the category of fruits and vegtables can have additional specific items. These would be nested under each heading.

for example

  • fruit
    • apples
    • bananas
    • strawberries
    • pomegranate
  • vegtables
    • onions
    • brocolli
    • carrots
    • garlic
    • celery
    • cabbage

The instructions for baking the cake, could also have nested sections.

for example

  1. Preheat oven to 350°.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk:
    • flour
    • sugar
    • baking cocoa
    • baking powder
    • salt
  3. stir in
    • eggs
    • oil
    • butter
  4. add water, stir until mixture is like a dough
